Lasagna

Ultimate Lasagna Bolgnese

Rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel and loads of mozzarella make up this ultimate lasagna

Ingredients

Meat

  • 34 oz Ground beef or you could use a mix of ground pork and beef

Produce

  • 1 Carrot
  • 1 Celery
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1 Parsley or basil, fresh
  • 24 oz Passata or strained tomatoes
  • 14 oz Tomatoes

Canned Goods

  • 2 Beef or vegetable bouillon cubes
  • 3 heaping tbsp Tomato paste

Condiments

  • 1 tsp Sugar or honey
  • tsp Worcestershire sauce

Pasta & Grains

  • 3 oz Lasagna, fresh

Baking & Spices

  • 1/4 cup All purpose flour
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning blend
  • 1 Pinch Nutmeg or clove
  • 2 Salt and pepper

Oils & Vinegar

  • 2 tbsp Olive oil

Dairy

  • 4 tbsp Butter
  • 17 oz Mozzarella cheese, fresh
  • cup Parmesan, fresh grated

Beer, Wine, Liquor

  • 1/4 cup Red wine

Other

  • 3½ cups while milk room temperature or warmed slightly

Steps

  1. In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium low heat. Add flour and whisk constantly for 1 minute.
  2. Pour approx 1 cup of the milk in, whisking the whole time. Once mostly lump free, whisk in the remaining milk.
  3. Turn heat up to medium high. Whisk occasionally at first then regularly after a few minutes until sauce thickens - about 5 - 8 minutes. It should coat the back of the wooden spoon.
  4. Remove from heat, add cheese, nutmeg, salt and pepper. Mix until the cheese is melted. The sauce should be thick but still easily pourable - If it’s too thick, add whisk in a little extra milk.
  5. Preheat oven to 350°F
  6. Spoon and spread approx 1 cup of the meat sauce at the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish, then cover with pasta sheets. Trimming sheets if needed. Layer with 2 cups of meat sauce (or enough to cover pasta), 1 cup of béchamel sauce spread over the meat sauce and then sprinkle approx ½ cup of mozzarella over top. Repeat layers making sure you have a good amount of cheese left for the top layer.
  7. For the last layer – cover with lasagna sheets, pour over remaining meat sauce followed by the remaining béchamel sauce and then sprinkle with remaining cheese. Place on a large baking sheet to catch any bubbling cheese and mess and bake for 25 minutes or until golden and bubbling.
  8. Remove from oven and let stand 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving. Serve garnished with chopped fresh parsley or basil. Leftovers keep for up to five days if stored in a tightly sealed container to keep out excess moisture and other contaminants. Reheat in oven or microwave.
